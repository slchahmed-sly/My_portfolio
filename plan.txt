Frontend Design Specifications - Phase 4 (The Blog & Publication)
1. Page Architecture: The Feed (/blog)
Layout Style: "Clean List" (Vertical flow of horizontal cards).

Header:

Title: "Writing" or "Journal".

Search Input: A clean, wide search bar to filter posts by title/content in real-time.

Tag Cloud: A scrollable row of pill-buttons (e.g., Django, Deep Learning, Math) to filter by topic.

The Article Card (Horizontal Design)
Layout: Split content (Text Left, Image Right).

Left (Content):

Date & Read Time: Small, grey text (e.g., "Jan 12, 2026 • 6 min read").

Title: Bold, clickable link (H2).

Excerpt: 2 lines of text, truncated.

Right (Visual):

Thumbnail: A small, square (1:1) or rectangular (4:3) image.

Responsive: On mobile, the image moves to the top (or disappears to save space), stacking the content vertically.

2. Page Architecture: The Article (/blog/:slug)
Layout: Two-Column Layout (Desktop).

Left/Center (65%): The Article Content.

Right (25%): Sticky Sidebar (Table of Contents).

Mobile Layout: Single column. The TOC collapses into a "Map" button at the top or bottom.

Component Breakdown
A. The Article Header

Meta Info: Published Date • Read Time (Calculated automatically based on word count).

Title: Massive H1 typography.

Tags: List of relevant topics.

Cover Image: Full-width, high-quality banner.

B. The Sidebar (Table of Contents)

Behavior: Sticky. As the user scrolls down the article, this menu stays fixed on the right side.

Interactivity:

Lists all H2 and H3 headers from the markdown.

Active State: The current section being read is highlighted (e.g., Blue text/border).

Clicking: Smooth scrolls to that section.

C. The Content Body

Typography: Optimized for long-form reading (larger font size text-lg, relaxed leading-relaxed).

Engine: react-markdown + rehype-katex (Math) + syntax-highlighter (Code).

Special Styling: Blockquotes (border-left), Lists (properly indented), Images (centered with captions).

D. The Comment Section

Position: Bottom of the article.

Form: Simple inputs for Name and Message.

Submit Action: Sends POST request to Django.

Feedback: "Comment submitted for approval."

List: Displays approved comments with Date and Name.
